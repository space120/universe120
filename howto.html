How to use Universe120

Quick start: Select the Line tool → click to start → move → click to end. Hold Space to pan, use the mouse wheel to zoom. Smart badges show angles (0/30/45/60/90°), equal lengths (≡), parallel (∥), and perpendicular (⊥).

The Bubble Grid

The grid is made of overlapping circles (like blueprint dots). Intersections are natural snap points—great for squares, rooms, bridges, and arcs. Change size with Grid and Dots sliders in the top bar.

Tools

  • Select — click shapes to select; Shift-click to multi-select. Drag to move; use the yellow handle to rotate (snaps to common angles).
  • Line — straight segments; angle-lock helps you hit exact orientations.
  • Pen (Polyline) — chain lines into a path; Enter to finish, Backspace to undo last point.
  • Rectangle — drag; it shows a square badge when width=height.
  • Arc — two clicks define the endpoints; the curve is centered between them.
  • Circle — center then radius.
  • Text — place labels like “Kitchen 12’ x 10’”.
  • Dimension — click A then B to place a measurement label.
  • Erase — removes the nearest shape to your cursor.

Smart Guides & Snapping

Set Snap (Intersections/Centers/Midpoints/Free) and Angle (Off, 0/90°, 30/60/90°, 45/90°). While drawing, badges appear near your cursor with angle and length hints. Only the two most important badges show so it stays readable.

Moving & Rotating

With Select, drag any shape (or group) to move. The move snaps to grid points and other anchor points (ends, centers, mids). Rotate using the yellow handle above the selection; it snaps to helpful angles.

Groups & Export

  • Group / Ungroup from the top bar—useful for rooms or symbols.
  • Export PNG for sharing; Export SVG for laser cutters or vector apps.

Keyboard shortcuts

  • Pan: hold Space and drag (or middle-mouse)
  • Zoom: wheel (zooms to cursor)
  • Undo/Redo: Ctrl/Cmd+Z / Ctrl/Cmd+Y
  • Reset 100%: 1 • Zoom Fit: Shift+1
  • Tools: V Select, L Line, P Pen, R Rect, A Arc, O Circle, T Text, D Dim, E Erase

Tips for classrooms

  • Have students draw a perfect square with smart guides, then calculate area/perimeter.
  • Design a tiny room with dimensions and labels; export SVG for printing.
  • Build a simple bridge truss using equal-length and parallel guides.
