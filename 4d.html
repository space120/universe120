4D Shapes — Blueprint View

4D shapes aren’t “seen” directly. We study them by projection (shadow into 2D/3D) and by cross-sections (slices that change over a 4th axis).

What is a 4D shape?

A 4D (four-dimensional) shape extends our 3D geometry with one extra perpendicular direction (call it W). Just like a cube is made from squares “extruded” along a new direction (Z), a tesseract (4D cube) can be thought of as a cube extruded along W. We explore 4D via:

  • Projection: Draw a wireframe in 2D/3D that preserves relationships (parallel, perpendicular, equal edges) as much as possible.
  • Slicing: Watch how a 3D slice grows and shrinks as you move along W (like a CT scan in medicine).

Classic 4D Regular Polytopes

Hypercube (Tesseract)

Cube → cube, connected corner-to-corner. That’s the standard tesseract wireframe.

Sections: slicing along W gives a sequence of 3D shapes that expand then contract.

16-Cell (Orthoplex)

Dual of the hypercube; in projection it resembles an “X+” star.

24-Cell

Unique to 4D: a regular polytope with 24 octahedral cells.

120-Cell / 600-Cell

Rich 4D analogs of dodecahedron/icosahedron; dense, beautiful projections.

How Universe120 will teach 4D (roadmap)

  • Projection Builder: draw linked inner/outer cubes with smart angle locks (30/60/90) to compose a tesseract wireframe on your bubble grid.
  • Slice Mode: scrub a slider to watch a 3D section of the 4D object grow & shrink (like a CT scan).
  • Annotate: use Text + Dimensions to label “inner/outer” faces and show changing measures.
